About Me

I received my PhD in Social Psychology from the University of Michigan in 2016. Since then, I have been working as a postdoctoral scholar at the University of Pennsylvania and U.S. Army Research Laboratory. I am currently the Robert J. Eichelberger Distinguished Postdoctoral Fellow in the Human Sciences Campaign at the U.S. Army Research Laboratory and a Visiting Scholar in the Bioengineering Department at the University of Pennsylvania.

I am a stats nerd and am passionate about using data to answer burning questions about how and why people do the things they do. Whether this is understanding cultural differences in decision-making, identifying patterns in human social interaction that predict team performance, or figuring out why Michigan football's offense is so disappointing (see my blog), I love using data to derive insights about human behavior.
